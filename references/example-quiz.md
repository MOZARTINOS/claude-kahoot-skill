# Example quiz — format reference

A short, topic-neutral example showing the target format and a balanced question mix. A real quiz would have 20–25 questions on whatever topic the user asks for. This is the companion document the host keeps; explanations are read aloud since Kahoot can't store them.

Question 1 (identification): Which planet is the largest in our solar system?
A. Earth   B. Mars   C. Jupiter   D. Venus
Correct answer: C
Explanation: Jupiter is by far the largest — more than 1,300 Earths could fit inside it.

Question 2 (core knowledge): What gas do plants mainly absorb from the air to grow?
A. Oxygen   B. Carbon dioxide   C. Helium   D. Nitrogen
Correct answer: B
Explanation: Plants take in carbon dioxide and release oxygen during photosynthesis.

Question 3 (famous thing): The Mona Lisa was painted by which artist?
A. Leonardo da Vinci   B. Picasso   C. Van Gogh   D. Michelangelo
Correct answer: A
Explanation: Leonardo da Vinci painted it in the early 1500s; it hangs in the Louvre.

Question 4 (simple logic / math): How many sides does a hexagon have?
A. 5   B. 6   C. 7   D. 8
Correct answer: B
Explanation: "Hexa" means six — a hexagon has six sides, like a honeycomb cell.

Question 5 (funny / guessable): Which of these is a REAL animal?
A. The narwhal   B. The jackalope   C. The griffin   D. The hydra
Correct answer: A
Explanation: The narwhal is a real Arctic whale with a long spiral tusk; the others are mythical.

## Tie-breakers (asked verbally if teams are level)

2. How many minutes are in a full day? — 1,440
3. What is the boiling point of water at sea level, in °C? — 100
4. How many colours are in a rainbow? — 7
5. What is the largest ocean on Earth? — The Pacific
